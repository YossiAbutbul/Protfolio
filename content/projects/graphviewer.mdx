## The problem

Antenna radiation patterns come out of test setups as plain `.txt` files. Useful, but unshareable — anyone who wants a look has to open the right tool, know the format, and squint. The lab needed a lightweight viewer that anyone could point a browser at.

## What I built

A zero-dependency HTML/CSS/JS viewer with Chart.js at its core. Drop in a `.txt` export, get an interactive polar + cartesian plot you can hover, zoom, and compare. No build step, no server.

## Highlights

- **File parsing in the browser** — handles the quirks of the lab's export format.
- **Chart.js-driven plots** — polar + cartesian side by side.
- **Shareable** — ship one HTML page with the data inline, send a link.

## What I learned

Sometimes the right amount of engineering is less engineering. A static page beats a framework when the audience is your bench partner and the asset lifetime is measured in hours.
